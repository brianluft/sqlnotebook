DATETIMEOFFSETFROMPARTS Function

Returns a string formatted like "2015-12-25 06:54:47.152 -04:00" based on the provided date, time, and time zone offset values.

Syntax

DATETIMEOFFSETFROMPARTS(year, month, day, hour, minute, second, millisecond, hour-offset, minute-offset)

Arguments

  • year (integer expression)
    Four-digit year.
  • month (integer expression, 1-12)
    Calendar month specified as a 1-based integer.
  • day (integer expression, 1-31)
    Day of the month specified as a 1-based integer.
  • hour (integer expression, 0-23)
    Clock hour expressed as a 0-based integer in 24-hour time.
  • minute (integer expression, 0-59)
    Clock minute.
  • second (integer expression, 0-59)
    Clock second.
  • millisecond (integer expression, 0-999)
    Clock millisecond.
  • hour-offset (integer expression)
    The hour part of the time zone offset. May be positive or negative.
  • minute-offset (integer expression)
    The minute part of the time zone offset. The sign of minute-offset must match the sign of hour-offset, or else an incorrect time zone offset will be calculated.

Return Value

A date/time string with time zone offset formatted like "2015-01-02 05:45:30.123 -04:00".

Examples

  • PRINT DATETIMEOFFSETFROMPARTS(2015, 1, 2, 5, 45, 30, 123, -5, -30);
    Prints "2015-01-02 05:45:30.123 -05:30".
